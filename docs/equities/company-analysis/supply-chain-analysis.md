# SPLC - Supply Chain Analysis

SPLC is Bloomberg's supply chain analysis page. It maps a company's suppliers and customers and breaks down its geographic, commodity, sustainability, and risk exposures.

## Use SPLC For

- Identifying a company's largest suppliers and customers, and how much - business each represents
- Tracking geographic and geopolitical exposure across the supply chain
- Spotting credit, sanctions, and physical risks among counterparties
- Assessing commodity and material exposure for cost-trend forecasting
- Evaluating ESG and sustainability risk across the supplier network
## Toolbar Options
After loading a security (e.g., NVDA US Equity), three Basic Parameters appear above the tab strip:
- Filter: Narrow the data by domicile, sector, or market cap.
- As of: Set a current or historical date.
- Currency: Choose the display currency, or FRC to use the company's fundamental reporting currency.

## 1. Summary

Dashboard view of the entire supply network. Covers top geographies, risk flags, commodity exposures, sustainability insights, peer comparison, and the ranked list of suppliers and customers with relationship size.

## 2. Key Metrics

Shortcut: `SPLC TABLE`. A filterable table of suppliers or customers with relationship-level detail. Toggle suppliers vs. customers at the upper left.
- Cost Category: How the relationship is accounted for — COGS, CAPEX, R&D, or SG&A.
- % Cost / % Revenue: Share of the central company's costs (suppliers) or the related company's revenue (customers) that the relationship represents.
- Source: Whether the data point is reported, estimated by Bloomberg, or modeled.

## 3. Geo-Exposure

Shortcut: `SPLC GEO`. Groups suppliers or customers by country/region. Click a row to see counterparties with facilities there, regardless of legal domicile — useful for geopolitical exposure analysis.

## 4. Risks

Shortcut: `SPLC RISK`. Surfaces credit, physical, and sanctions risk across the network.
- Default Risk: Bloomberg's default probability for each counterparty.
- Default Risk Grade: Letter-grade equivalent (IG1–IG10 investment grade, HY1–HY6 high yield, distressed grades below).
- Total Sanctions: Count of sanctions lists the counterparty appears on.

## 5. Commodities/Materials

Shortcut: `SPLC COM`. Publicly disclosed commodity and material exposures with recent price changes. Click a row to see exposed companies; click the chart icon for the commodity ticker.

## 6. Sustainability

Shortcut: `SPLC ESG`. ESG percentile, controversy risk, physical risk, and water stress for each counterparty. Click any score to launch ESG analysis.

## Other SPLC Notes

- Coverage: 28,000+ public companies and 94,000+ private companies.
- US disclosure: SEC Reg S-K only requires disclosure of customers >10% of revenue, annually. No supplier disclosure rule. Bloomberg fills gaps with mathematically and algorithmically derived data.
- Quantified relationships expire after ~14 months without a refresh.
